The Problem
HR leaders and legal ops teams at companies expanding globally face a persistent challenge: every country has different employment laws, mandatory benefits, notice periods, and termination rules. Getting answers requires expensive legal counsel, weeks of research, or trusting outdated internal documentation.

Key Requirements
Wireframe Annotations
1
Country + Employment Type selectors first — constrains the query scope before natural language input. Reduces hallucination risk and improves compliance specificity. HR leaders think in terms of jurisdiction first.
2
Natural language input, not form fields — the job-to-be-done is asking a question, not filling a form. Removes cognitive load and mirrors how people actually think about compliance problems.
3
Two-column response layout — full compliance narrative on the left, key requirements summary on the right. Serves two users simultaneously: the HR generalist who reads everything, and the legal ops professional who scans for specifics.
4
No account creation on first use — activation happens through value delivery, not registration friction. PQL trigger fires after third query — consistent with product-led growth principles.
Product Thinking
Key Product Decisions
A record of the decisions made during product development — the trade-offs considered and the reasoning behind each choice.
1
Country + type selectors before free text
Alternative considered: pure natural language input only. Rejected because compliance answers without jurisdictional context are unreliable. Structured selectors constrain the query space and improve accuracy. Trade-off: slightly more friction on input, significantly higher output quality.
2
Plain language output, not legal citation format
HR leaders are not lawyers. Responses written in accessible language with clear structure outperform dense legal prose for activation and retention. Legal citations available on request — not forced on every output.
3
No authentication wall on first use
Self-serve activation model — user experiences value before being asked to register. Registration triggered after demonstrable engagement. PQL signal: user saves or copies a response.
4
Scope limited to employment compliance only
Deliberate scope constraint. Reduces liability, improves model accuracy, and sharpens the product's value proposition. Does one thing perfectly rather than doing everything adequately.
5
Disclaimer on every response
Not optional. Compliance context requires explicit framing that responses are guidance, not legal advice. Builds trust rather than eroding it — users who trust the boundaries of a tool use it more, not less.
